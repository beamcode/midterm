Japanese Experience

Published 17 May 2023


Why did we chose Japan?


Japan's influence over the other countries is growing fast, for example, as time goes on, more and more Japanese restaurants like sushi restaurants open in Europe or America, more mangas are being translated in other languages than japanese and being sold in regular bookstores.
All of these factors together make that all members of our group were influenced by japanese culture and, as we had the chance to study abroad, we chose to study in Japan.

What we like in Japan


  • Japanese food: Before tavelling to Japan, we all tasted to Japanese dishes such as suchis or ramens, but here, everything is so much tastier and cheaper, it's awesome! And there are far more Japanese specialities than I would have thought (curry, yakitori, yakuniku, melon pan, mochi, daikufu, dango, tempura, ...).
  • Konbinis: There are so useful, it's amazing. You can find everyting there at a cheap price. Moreover they're always open they're everywhere.
  • IC cards (pasmo, suica, ...): An other handy thing, it acts as your bus ticket but also as little debit card. It's surprising to see in how much stores you can pay with it.
  • Cost of life: Everything is cheap!

What we don't like in Japan


  • Language barrier: Only a few people here speak English well here, and as we don't talk Japanese neither, it's sometimes hard to understand each other.
  • Earthquakes: Apart from being very dangerous when they're strong, they wake you up at 4am and make things at height fall down.
  • No trashcans: It's annoying.
  • Politicians in election period: They drive in the street with their megaphone and wake you up early, it's annying too.

Our opinion on Japan


Apart from the few small downsides which are greatly overweight by the upsides, Japan is great!
